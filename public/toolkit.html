Recommended Tracking Apps

These free apps complement your course beautifully. Use them alongside your workbook to track your progress — then bring your data to your AI coach for guidance.

MyFitnessPal
Nutrition & food logging
The gold standard for tracking what you eat. Huge food database, barcode scanner, macro tracking, and recipe saving. Use it to log meals and review your why, when, and what patterns from the course.
Cronometer
Detailed micronutrient tracking
Goes deeper than calories — tracks vitamins, minerals, and micronutrients. Ideal if you want to ensure your rainbow of whole foods is truly nourishing you at a cellular level.
Bearable
Mood, energy & whole-person wellness
Tracks mood, energy, sleep, symptoms, and habits in one place — perfectly aligned with Tina's tri-dimensional approach. Use it to chart your mood and endurance progress alongside your workbook charts.
Daylio
Simple mood & habit journaling
A quick, low-friction daily mood and habit tracker. Great for students who want a simple digital version of the journaling prompts in the workbook.
Strong
Workout & strength logging
Tracks your exercises, weights, reps, and progress over time. Pairs well with the isometric and resistance exercises from the Recreational Movement module.
Strava
Cardio & endurance tracking
Best for walking, running, hiking, biking, and swimming. Tracks distance, time, and elevation — great for logging your endurance improvements over 30, 60, and 90 days.

Clean Shopping Guide

Tina's golden rule: shop the perimeter of the grocery store. Perishable whole foods live on the edges. Processed, shelf-stable products live in the centre aisles.

The EWG Dirty Dozen & Clean 15

The Environmental Working Group publishes an annual list of which produce has the most and least pesticide residue. Check it every year at ewg.org/foodnews — it changes annually.

  • Dirty Dozen — always buy organic for these (high pesticide absorption into the edible part)
  • Clean 15 — conventional is acceptable for these (low pesticide absorption)
  • When you must choose between a non-organic fruit/veg and a processed food — always choose the fruit or vegetable
Reading Labels
  • Can you pronounce every ingredient? If not, look it up
  • Avoid anything ending in -ol (xylitol, erythritol, sorbitol) — sugar alcohols harm your gut biome
  • Look for "non-GMO" and "organic" certifications on grains
  • Check supplement labels — what are the fillers? Are they recognisable?
  • Shorter ingredient lists are almost always better
What to Avoid
  • GMO grains — Roundup-resistant crops carry pesticide residue directly into your gut biome
  • Refined sugar — feeds bad gut bacteria, causes glycemic spikes, drives cravings
  • Artificial sweeteners — sugar alcohols (-ol endings) harm the gut biome more than they help
  • Processed / packaged foods — chemical processing destroys nutrients and toxifies your gut
  • Sodas — terrible chemical composition; even diet sodas via artificial sweeteners
Better Sweetener Alternatives
  • Best: pure stevia extract (no sugar alcohols added) · pure monk fruit extract
  • Occasional treats: maple sugar · date sugar · coconut sugar — in moderation

Stress Management Quick Reference

Elevated cortisol from unmanaged stress is one of the most common hidden barriers to weight loss. These techniques come directly from Tina's course — find what works for you.

Tina's #1 Technique — Deep Breathing with Scripture
  • Breathe in slowly and deeply, filling your diaphragm completely until your belly expands, then your chest
  • Hold for 4 seconds
  • As you inhale, receive God's presence — His peace, His truth about who you are
  • Exhale very slowly, releasing your stressors into His hands
  • Repeat 7-10 times. This oxygenates your bloodstream and acts as a natural muscle relaxant
The 5-Minute Reset
  • Breathe deeply
  • Brain dump, prayer, or meditative listening for God's encouragement
  • Prioritize one next beneficial thing
Additional Techniques
  • Write stress onto paper strips, submerge in water — physically release the tension
  • Write a stress letter, shred it, put it in your shoes, go for a brisk walk
  • Transfer stress energy into gardening, vigorous housework, or exercise
  • Work frustrations out through clay, art, or creative mediums
  • Foot soak in warm saltwater — work stress out through your toes
  • Tapping (EFT) exercises — especially helpful for anxiety and emotional eating urges
  • Prayer: vent concerns to God, submit them, listen, meditate on scripture

Sleep Hygiene Checklist

Quality sleep is essential for metabolism, hormone regulation, and weight loss. Click each item to check it off — and identify what to work on next.

  • Finish eating at least 3 hours before bedtime
  • Avoid caffeine during the second half of the day
  • Turn off blue light screens 60-90 minutes before bed
  • Create a cool, dark sleep environment
  • Block noise (inexpensive earplugs work very well)
  • Evict work and stress triggers from your sleeping area
  • Move phone away from bedside; turn off router if possible
  • Limit naps to 10-15 minutes during the day
  • Exercise earlier in the day, not right before bedtime
  • Maintain consistent bedtime and wake time
  • Build a daily wind-down routine (bath, foot soak, herbal tea)
  • Practice relaxation breathing with prayer before sleep
  • Place to-do lists and concerns in your Bible before bed
  • Consider calming teas: chamomile, lavender, lemon balm, passionflower
  • Dim lights early to allow natural melatonin to develop

If you struggle with sleep despite good hygiene, ask your doctor about a sleep study. Undiagnosed sleep apnea is a surprisingly common hidden barrier to weight loss.

Accountability Partner Guide

One of the most powerful things you can do for your wellness journey is invite the right person to walk alongside you. Here's how to do it well.

Choosing Your Partner
  • Choose someone who supports your values and aligns with your schedule
  • They should be willing to give BOTH honest AND encouraging feedback
  • Avoid someone who struggles with their own relationship with food — they may unconsciously sabotage you
  • A life coach, mentor, or trusted friend in a faith community are all great options
  • Tina Yeager offers personal coaching at tinayeager.com
The Check-In Structure
  • Weekly 10-20 minute check-ins for the first 12 weeks
  • Then 2x per month as habits become more established
  • At each check-in discuss: Wins · Hurdles / setbacks · Next steps
Your Support Circles
  • Inner circle — most trusted; direct authority to speak into your life; must be life-giving and values-aligned
  • Middle circle — close friends and acquaintances; positive and supportive but less direct influence
  • Outer circle — broader acquaintances; monitor how much influence you allow from those whose relationship with food or emotional health is still broken

"The people around you are great determiners of where you are going. If you find yourself surrounded by people not going where you want to go, add in some different people."

— Tina Yeager
